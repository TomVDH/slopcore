# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People the maker shares this with for fun: colleagues at ZenaTech and friends in on
the joke. They open it on a desktop or phone with no instructions, rip a few packs
out of curiosity, and either bounce after one pull or get pulled into completing the
album. There is no task to finish and nothing to learn; the only job is "amuse me and
make me want one more."

## Product Purpose

A single-use, no-backend web toy: rip a shimmering foil pack, reveal which nation you
pulled, read its lore, and collect all 48. The running gag is that it is the same man,
"Marcus Masterton" of ZENA FC, reskinned as a national-stereotype caricature for every
country, each with a localized pun name. Success is the unboxing feeling genuinely good:
the rip is satisfying, the reveal earns a smile, and the album makes you want the set.

## Brand Personality

Premium-but-playful. Three words: irreverent, crafted, collectible. The voice is
affectionate mock-Panini: the joke is always on Marcus the over-eager journeyman, never
the nations. The interface is a dark gallery wall ("Wall Text") that hangs a silly object
and takes it completely seriously — museum wall-label deadpan, not a casino trying to
extract a coin.

## Anti-references

- Casino / loot-box garishness: spinning slot reels, coin showers, flashing JACKPOT,
  aggressive timers or pressure. Restraint signals quality; this is a toy, not a gacha mill.
- Generic AI-slop: purple-to-blue hero gradients, glassmorphism everywhere, three equal
  feature cards, neon-on-black "AI dashboard" look, eyebrow kickers above every block.
- Recoloring or "improving" the card art. The printed red Panini frame is sacred; the UI
  frames it, it does not redecorate it.

## Design Principles

- **The card is the only loud thing.** The chrome stays quiet — a flat gallery wall (dark by
  default, light variant available) — so the saturated red sticker is the single point of
  colour; rarity hues appear only at the moment of reveal.
- **Earn the reveal, then get out of the way.** One orchestrated unboxing moment carries the
  delight; everything after settles into a calm, legible resting state.
- **The sidebar is a museum placard.** Every lore block must add something the card image
  cannot (decoded pun name, comedic stint, props, reframed stats), never restate it.
- **Delight is a moment, not a coat of paint.** Motion, foil and sparkle concentrate on the
  rip and reveal; the album and lore are quiet by comparison.
- **Joke up, never down.** Affection for the nations, mockery reserved for Marcus.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast throughout. First-class `prefers-reduced-motion` path (instant cross-fade,
no particles, no tilt, no idle motion). Full keyboard operation (rip, re-roll, album roving
focus, sidebar dismiss), visible ultramarine focus rings, rich per-card alt text, and an `aria-live`
announcer on every pull. Rarity is never conveyed by colour alone (always the tier name +
odds). A future "reduce glare" toggle is noted for photosensitivity.
